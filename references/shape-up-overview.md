# Shape Up — Overview

Shape Up is a product development methodology from Basecamp, documented by Ryan Singer at https://basecamp.com/shapeup.

## Cycle structure

- **6-week cycle** — the standard unit of committed work. Teams build one shaped pitch per cycle. At the end, it ships or it doesn't.
- **2-week cool-down** — between cycles. No committed work. Time for fixes, side quests, and shaping the next batch.

## Three phases

1. **Shaping** — senior people (product, tech lead) take raw ideas and turn them into **pitches**: problem + appetite + fat-marker solution + rabbit holes + no-gos. Shaping happens during cool-down or in parallel with the current cycle.
2. **Betting** — the betting table reviews shaped pitches and picks which ones to bet on for the next cycle. Not a backlog — pitches are either bet on or dropped. The cost of a bad pitch is low because the appetite cap limits damage.
3. **Building** — a small team (one developer + one designer, usually) owns a bet for a full cycle. They have autonomy to make scope choices. The hill chart tracks progress.

## Core disciplines

- **Appetite, not estimate.** Pitches declare the time we're willing to spend. If the work doesn't fit, we cut scope — we don't extend time.
- **Fat-marker, not wireframe.** Solutions stay at the place/affordance level during shaping. Detail happens during building.
- **Circuit breaker.** If a bet doesn't ship within its cycle, it doesn't ship. No extensions.
- **Scope-hammering.** Mid-cycle, the team cuts scope to fit the appetite.

## Anti-patterns it rejects

- Backlog grooming. Shape Up has no backlog — pitches are bet or dropped.
- Story-point estimation. Appetite is the budget; work fits or gets cut.
- Rolling milestones. Cycles are fixed; unfinished work is dropped, not rolled forward.
- Sprint-style planning. The 6-week cycle is the unit, not 2-week sprints.

## Anti-anti-patterns (things Shape Up is NOT)

- Not anti-planning — shaping IS the plan.
- Not anti-quality — the appetite cap forces trade-offs that improve focus.
- Not anti-agile — it's an opinionated agile variant, not a waterfall revival.
