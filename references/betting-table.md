# Betting Table

*Used by the (planned) `shapeup:bet` skill for cycle selection.*

The **betting table** is the Shape Up meeting where senior people (product, tech leads, sometimes founders) decide which shaped pitches to bet on for the next cycle. It happens once per cycle — typically at the end of cool-down.

## What goes into the meeting

- **Shaped pitches** — pitches that passed the shaping discipline (problem, appetite, fat-marker solution, rabbit holes, no-gos).
- **Nothing else.** No half-shaped ideas, no "we should consider X," no spillover from last cycle. If it's not shaped, it doesn't make the table.

## What comes out

For each pitch reviewed, one of two outcomes:

- **Bet** — we'll build this next cycle. A team is assigned.
- **Drop** — not this cycle. The pitch goes back into the shaping pool (to be re-shaped, revised, or abandoned).

There is no "maybe." There is no backlog. Pitches that don't win a bet are not queued for "next time" — if they're still worth doing, they'll be re-shaped and re-submitted later.

## Why the binary matters

A backlog is a graveyard. Anything parked there accumulates context-rot — the problem changes, the solution ages, the rabbit holes shift. Shape Up's alternative is: shape fresh when you want to bet, drop otherwise. It feels wasteful; it's actually a filter that keeps only live problems in play.

## Composition

- Small group (3–5 people typically). Enough perspective, not enough for theater.
- Senior. The betting table is where costly decisions get made; it needs people who understand the cost.
- Same people every cycle (roughly). Continuity helps calibration.

## Time-box

Short. An hour or two. The shaping has already happened; the meeting is just yes/no per pitch.

## Signs it's working

- The meeting is quick because the pitches are well-shaped.
- Nobody is pitching unshaped ideas in the room.
- Dropped pitches aren't taken personally — they're a normal outcome.
- The team consistently ships the bets (because the appetites are real).

## Signs it's NOT working

- Pitches need extensive Q&A in the room — they weren't shaped enough.
- "Parking lot" ideas keep getting carried forward — the backlog is leaking in.
- Bets regularly fail the circuit breaker — the table is over-betting or under-shaping.
