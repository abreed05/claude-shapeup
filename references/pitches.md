# Pitches — Anatomy

A **pitch** is a shaped proposal for a bet. It is the output of the shaping phase and the input to the betting table.

## The five sections

1. **Problem** — a concrete situation someone is in. Includes raw material (quote, ticket, incident), the specific scenario (who, what, when, friction), and why-now.
2. **Appetite** — the time we're willing to spend. Small batch (1–2 weeks), big batch (6 weeks), or non-standard with written justification. Appetite is a constraint, not a prediction.
3. **Solution** — a fat-marker outline of the approach. Places, affordances, connection lines. Not wireframes. Includes a breadboard diagram.
4. **Rabbit holes** — identified risks, unknowns, and stuck-points. "None" is not accepted — if you truly see none, you haven't looked.
5. **No-gos** — explicit scope cuts. What we are NOT doing, and why. Tells the betting table the shape of the bet.

## What "shaped" means

A pitch is shaped when all five sections exist, pass the discipline (concrete problem, clear appetite, fat-marker solution, real rabbit holes, real no-gos), and a betting table could read it and make a yes/no decision in under ten minutes.

## What shaped is NOT

- Not a spec. No field-by-field detail, no wireframes, no API contracts.
- Not a ticket. Tickets come from scopes during the build phase.
- Not a plan. The team makes plans during building; shaping bounds the problem.
- Not exhaustive. A pitch is a concentration of the riskiest decisions — everything else is left to the building team's judgment.

## Signs a pitch isn't ready

- Appetite is missing or vague ("a few weeks").
- Problem is abstract ("users want better analytics").
- Solution has specific copy, colors, or API signatures.
- Rabbit holes say "none" or "tbd".
- No-gos say "none" or are missing.
- You can't explain the bet in one sentence.
